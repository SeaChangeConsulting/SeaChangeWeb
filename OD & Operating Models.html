The Network Operating Model

The modern business environment is defined by unprecedented complexity and a rapid pace of change, posing a defining challenge for organizations on how to profitably deliver services to customers. Customer expectations are rapidly changing, radically shifting profits and forcing executives to navigate an increasingly complex landscape of technologies and regulatory pressures. In this high-stakes environment, merely embarking on transformation journeys is not enough; research shows a competitive "power curve" where the top quintile of performers capture more than 90 percent of the economic profit.

To move up this curve and achieve the step-function improvement in productivity required, leaders must commit to reinventing their core business by putting in place a next-generation operating model that can sustain new levels of speed, agility, efficiency, and precision.

The Network Advantage

Organizations that adopt networked or agile structures report improvements in speed, innovation, and employee engagement. By aligning teams around value streams, a Network Operating model (NOM) eliminates internal barriers and bolsters cross-functional collaboration, ensuring that the best-suited experts address each problem regardless of their formal department.

Ultimately, the success of this transformation hinges on leadership commitment, as senior executives have an outsize influence on driving organizational alignment, championing new models of working, and anchoring the transformation through bold and effective decisions.
